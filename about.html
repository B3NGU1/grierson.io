Who I am

I'm a student based in Paris with a taste for small experiments. I like starting things, seeing what works, and learning from what doesn't. My interests sit at the intersection of sport, languages, and building things online.

Background

I spent four months in China — an internship at Schneider Electric and learning Mandarin from scratch. Before that, I cycled from the south of France to Spain, ran calisthenics and wellness accounts on social media, and tried selling websites to small businesses. Some of these worked. Most taught me something.

I'm currently working a data internship at JCDecaux in Paris.

What I'm doing now

  • Learning German daily (Migaku + Pingo AI)
  • Building things with code — habit trackers, tools, games
  • Working toward my first online income
  • Designing a lifestyle around location independence, learning, and small bets

How I think

I take a lot from Tim Ferriss — run small experiments, measure what matters, discard what doesn't work, double down on what does. I'd rather try ten things and learn from seven failures than spend months planning the perfect one.

I believe the best way to learn is to build. The best way to build is to ship. The best way to ship is to start before you're ready.
